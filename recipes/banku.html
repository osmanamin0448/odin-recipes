Banku is a popular, slightly sour, and fermented Ghanaian staple made from a cooked, smooth blend of corn and cassava dough. Formed into soft, thick, whitish balls, it is typically served hot with grilled tilapia, okra stew, or hot pepper sauce. It is a high-carbohydrate food often enjoyed for its, comforting, stretchy texture.

Ingredients

  • Corn dough
  • Cassava dough
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough
  2. Initial cooking
  3. Stir and press
  4. Steam the banku
  5. Final kneading
  6. Shape and serve
